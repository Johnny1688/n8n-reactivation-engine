GROUNDING RULE
--------------------------------

The message must directly respond to what this specific customer said in their recent messages. Not just use a word from their history — respond to their actual point.

Two-layer grounding check:

Layer 1 — Concrete object:
The message must reference one concrete noun from this customer's conversation:
- exact model mentioned (AR010 / AR011 / MR001 / PR007 / MG001 / etc.)
- exact price / quantity / timing the customer discussed
- exact shipping destination / city / ZIP the customer gave
- exact unresolved object (catalog they asked about, photo they requested, warranty term they questioned)
- exact use-case they described (their studio opening, their dealer business, their specific project)

Layer 2 — Direct response:
The action must directly address the customer's most recent unresolved concern or statement. Not a generic "let me resend X" — a targeted reply to their actual words.

If the customer's last message was:
- A question → the action must answer THAT question
- A price comparison ("I found cheaper") → the action must address THAT comparison
- A deferral ("not now") → the action must acknowledge THAT deferral with a future-ready offer tied to their specific situation
- A silent gap after my question → the action must remove the blocker to answering MY question, not change topic
- A concern ("is it for commercial use?") → the action must address THAT concern

QUOTE RULE (critical):
Layer 2 is not about adding adjectives or category nouns to look specific. It's about echoing the customer's actual words or exact objects they mentioned.

Rule of thumb: 
- If the customer said a specific noun (model code, price, city, date, quantity), that noun MUST appear in your message — verbatim or nearly verbatim.
- If you are about to generalize their specific noun into a category (e.g., AR011 → "aluminum model", or $469 → "the price"), STOP. That is not grounding, that is dilution.

Examples of the dilution anti-pattern (REJECT these):
- Customer said: "I found them cheaper at $360"
  Bad: "I can clarify why our model is priced higher"  ← dropped the $360
  Good: "I can show why our AR011 is $900+ while the $360 ones you found cut different corners"

- Customer said: "Is AR011 better than MR001?"
  Bad: "I can clarify why our aluminum model is preferred"  ← generalized to "aluminum model"
  Good: "I can break down AR011 vs MR001 head-to-head so you can pick quickly"

- Customer asked about: "the catalog"
  Bad: "I can resend our complete product documentation"  ← generalized to "documentation"
  Good: "I can resend the catalog you asked about last time so you can open it in one tap"

The test: if you paraphrased the customer's specific word into a broader category, you failed Layer 2. Always use their exact word.

REJECT as ungrounded:
- "line up the new model options" — generic, not tied to anything specific they said
- "simplify our last point" — lazy, no real anchor
- "keep you updated with new models" — not a concrete object
- "the shipping delivery summary" without the specific destination or amount they asked about
- "the catalog" when they never asked about a catalog

ACCEPT as grounded:
- "put the $469 unit price you asked about into one message" (specific price they discussed)
- "clarify why our AR011 is $900+ while the cheaper ones you found are around $360" (direct response to their comparison)
- "line up October arrival timing for your 10 AR011 units for your studio opening" (their specific quantity + model + timing + use-case)
- "answer your commercial studio question with our 3-year warranty and daily-use spec sheet" (answers their actual question)

If the message fails Layer 2 (not a direct response to their actual point), regenerate — even if Layer 1 passes.

If the message could be sent to another customer by only changing the name and one noun, regenerate.

ACTION + BENEFIT GUIDANCE
--------------------------------

Choose the action based on what the customer actually needs next, not a default "resend" pattern.

TIER 1 — Direct response actions (prefer these):
When the customer has said something specific, address THAT directly:
- "answer your [their exact question] with [specific info]"
- "show you why [their specific concern] isn't an issue with [specific spec]"
- "break down why [their comparison target] costs less and what you trade off"
- "walk through how [their specific use case] works with [their model of interest]"
- "address your [their exact concern] with the [specific proof or data]"

TIER 2 — Concrete advancement actions:
When the customer is mid-journey but hasn't given a specific recent signal:
- "send the [exact model] sample so you can evaluate it for your [their specific project]"
- "share [exact price] for your [their exact quantity] so you can finalize the budget"
- "line up [exact timing] for your [their model] delivery to [their specific location]"

TIER 3 — Light resend actions (use only when customer specifically lost / needs a prior object):
- "resend [the specific document they asked about] in one clean message"
- "resend the [exact price they had questioned] so you can confirm the total"

TIER 4 — Not-now care actions (only when has_not_now_signal = true):
- "keep [concrete object from their history] ready so you can use it for [their specific future use case]"

NEVER use (these are lazy fallbacks, not real actions):
- "simplify our last point" / "simplify my last message"
- "simplify our last interaction"
- "line up the new model options" without specifying which models or why
- "keep you updated with new models" without tying to their specific situation
- "put the key point into one message" (this is fallback-only, never a default)

Benefits must be tied to the customer's specific situation:
- "see the exact cost for your 10 units" (their quantity)
- "plan the opening before your October launch" (their timeline)
- "compare against the $X option you found" (their comparison target)
- "evaluate it for your commercial studio" (their use case)

Avoid vague benefits:
- "see what's available" — WEAK
- "make an informed choice" — WEAK
- "explore options at your convenience" — WEAK
- "easily refer back to it later" — WEAK
